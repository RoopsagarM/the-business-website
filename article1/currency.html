FOREIGN EXCHANGE V/S FALL OF RUPEE

  Siri Manda






US DOLLAR AS GLOBAL CURRENCY:


US Dollars are considered as Global currency as most of the countries do trades and purchases from the foreign countries across the world.US currency had become an international mode of transaction and other countries currency has been falling down. During the world war-2 most of the countries need weapons to face the war.As US had lots of weapons it made a deal with other countries by exchanging their weapons to the countries who need weapons by gold.By doing such deals and exchanges foreign countries had lots of Gold.





After some years other countries wanted to take back their Gold so that they can give whatever the foreign countries demand.The arrival of the Bretton Woods Agreement in which approximately delegates from 44 countries signed a treaty with the foreign countries such that in order to return the gold reserves, the other countries have to pay them in US dollars which indirectly states that the US dollar equals the value of gold. Today, more than 61% of all foreign bank reserves are denominated in U.S. dollars, according to the International Monetary Fund. Many of the reserves are in cash or U.S bonds such as U.S. Treasuries. Also, approximately 40% of the world’s debt is denominated in dollars.


BRETTON WOODS AGREEMENT:


The Bretton Woods agreement which was held in conference of all of the World War II Allied nations. It took place in Bretton Woods, New Hampshire in1944 which established a new global monetary system. The replacement of the gold standard with the U.S. dollar as the global currency was made due to this agreement between the foreign countries and other countries. This agreement established America as the dominant power in the world economy. After the agreement was signed, America was the only country with the ability to print US Dollars.





This agreement created the World Bank and the International Monetary Fund (IMF), U.S.-backed organizations that would monitor the new system.﻿Under the agreement,all other countries promised that their central banks would maintain fixed exchange rates between their currencies and the US currency dollar. If a country's currency value became weak compared with the dollar, the bank would buy its currency in foreign exchange markets.





All the members in this agreement agreed to avoid trade wars. For example,if the other countries wouldn't lower their currencies strictly to increase trade but these countries could regulate their currencies under certain conditions. They could also adjust their currency values to rebuild after a war.Before Bretton Woods, most countries followed the gold standard.That meant each country guaranteed that it would redeem its currency for its value in gold. After Bretton Woods agreement other countries agreed to redeem its currency for U.S. dollars, not gold. The United States held three-fourths of the world's supply of gold. No other country's currency had enough gold to back it as a replacement. The dollar's value was 1/35 of an ounce of gold.This agreement by Bretton Woods allowed the world to transition from a gold standard to a U.S. dollar standard. Now,The dollar has become a substitute for gold. As a result, the value of the dollar began to increase relative to other countries' currencies.This agreement created more demand for dollars, even though its worth in gold remained the same.





RISE AND FALL OF INDIAN RUPEE:


The Rupee value is not only stable sometimes but also the value of rupee reached an all-time low or all-time high.This happens because countries like America which have dollars as their currency have high demand.Some trade companies of America which were doing trade and established some companies in India have to invest some dollars for their exchanges and trades.When US loses some dollars the Indian rupee increases.But this situation occurs some times as India is the developing nation with less investments with foreign countries. India imports goods from other countries so that it should pay dollars to other countries which indirectly means that the rupee should be converted into the dollar.So, we are losing money and the other countries are gaining US dollars. In this situation, it leads to the fall of rupee value. Reserve Bank Of India(RBI) has some techniques which stabilize the rupee value during its downfall. If the rupee value falls down, that means we are losing some Indian money.For example, India has 80 dollars and we have to give 60 dollars in return to the US for the goods that we have purchased. Then India will only have 20 dollars which lower the value of the rupee. At this time, the RBI gives 60 dollars from its foreign exchange reserves and now India will again have 80 dollars, so in this situation the value of rupee will be stable.The foreign reserves include gold, International Monetary funds, security bonds, etc.

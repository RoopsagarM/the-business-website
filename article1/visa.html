AMERICA PLANNED TO CANCEL H1-B VISA

  Siri Manda



The COVID-19 virus outbreak and the pandemic situation made the US-based anti-immigration technology workers group have asked for the H1-B visa program to be suspended due to growing joblessness.Donald Trump’s presidential proclamation suggests an idea on June 22, 2020,that if pursued and implemented, could drive many thousands of long-time H-1B visa holders out of the US . The plan, on the list of anti-immigration organizations, is to force foreign nationals waiting years for employment-based green cards to travel through the “labor certification” process again in the hopes many will not succeed. The regulation or policy about-face would aim to force highly skilled Indian, Chinese and Filipino nationals to go away from the US en bloc– in effect, deportation from America of many of the world’s most talented people.





US Tech Workers is a non-profit organization that has more local hiring and has written to US President Donald Trump mentioning that unemployment claims had gone up to 3 million in the past few weeks and with projections showing that this could rise further by 30 percent.“We urge you to pause the H-1B visa program which brings 85,000 workers in this year and suspend the recently approved additional 35,000 workers for the H-2B visa,” they wrote.





The H-1B visa is used primarily which brings the highly skilled workers to the US whereas the H-2B visa is mainly used for farmworkers, primarily from Latin America. The unemployment resulting from the pandemic has led to scores of people losing their jobs. In fact, unemployment rates have surpassed those ever known in history, according to the statistics provided by the United States Bureau of Labour.The IT industry association Nasscom declined to comment on US Tech Workers’ stance on H1-B visas although it pointed out that the H-1B visas “fulfill an acknowledged critical skills gap for all global companies in the US mark”.


Further, Nasscom has also requested the US Citizenship and Immigration Services for a 90-day grace period for tech professionals to leave the United States following the expiry of their visas, it said.Currently, the limit is 60 days. Citing the number of layoffs and economic uncertainty which were raised among the country, other organizations which represent tech workers have asked for it to be extended to 180 days. Indians have been the biggest beneficiaries of this program particularly which receives about 70 percent of the visas issued annually.





The share of H1-B visas allocated to Indian and US services companies has dropped significantly in the past few years.In the letter, US Tech Workers said “With the historical displacement of workers, compounded by the current crisis, it is very concerning that the Department of Homeland Security is working to accelerate the delivery of H-1B and H2-B workers to the United States. We ask that you stand up for the productive class of this country and protect the interest of American workers,”.


How Will This Affect?


The H-1B visa gives thousands of Asian tech workers mostly from India and China, the opportunity to work in the United States.According to the reports, Indian tech workers, who are a worldwide phenomenon, are one among the foremost benefited groups from the program.Further, the experts have predicted a worldwide recession due to coronavirus lockdown, which will affect most countries, including the United States. The US Department of Labour will be looking at 50 to 60 million unemployed Americans, as per the reports. So, the letter acknowledged that last Thursday theUS recorded its highest percentage in history after claims reached approximately three million, crossing the previous record of nearly 7,00,000 in October 1982.

A ​gas leakage that harks back to the 1984 Bhopal tragedy, have taken 11 lives and affected thousands of residents in visakhapatnam Andhra pradesh. It was due to the leakage of the gas styrene plant owned by South Korean electronics giant LG, located at RRV Puram near Gopalapatnam, about 15 kms from the coast city. Initial reports indicate that people from several surrounding villages (RRV Puram, Venkatapuram, BC Colony, Padmapuram and Kamparapalem) fell unconscious on the roads while six people died due to prolonged exposure of gas ,while other two died while escaping from the leak. A team of experts worked to neutralise the styrene gas at the chemical factory as well as in villages where it had spread. “It might take 48 hours to neutralise the gas everywhere,” Andhra Pradesh Industries Minister Goutam Reddy said. While 11 people died, 554 people were admitted to King George Hospital, and 128 have recovered completely, said Andhra Pradesh Health Minister A Krishna Srinivas. “There are 305 people still in hospital, including 52 children. All of them are out of danger and no one is on ventilators. About 121 others are receiving treatment at private hospitals, but all are out of danger,” he said.





Meanwhile Prime minister Narendra modi said he is praying for the safety of all those affected by a gas leak from a chemical plant in Andhra Pradesh's Visakhapatnam. And Andhra pradesh chief minister YS. jagan mohan reddy has announced 1 crore rupees for the compensation for the people who died and people who are getting treatment on ventilator would be given 10 lakh each , Those who have been treated as outpatients will be paid Rs 25,000 each.

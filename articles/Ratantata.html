RATAN TATA A BUSINESS KOHINOOR


O​ne of the most reputed generous tata sons chairman emeritus Ratan Naval Tata an industrialist and philanthropist and a former chairman of tata group of industries was born in bombay (Mumbai) on 28 dec 1937. His father naval tata and mother sonoo tata were separated in 1948 when he was 10. He was adopted through an orphanage J.N. petit parsi by his grandmother navajbai tata. He had done his schooling in Campion School, Mumbai till the 8th class, followed by Cathedral and John Connon School, Mumbai and at Bishop Cotton School in Shimla and he completed his twelfth grade in Riverdale Country School in New York City In 1959, he was undergraduate in architecture from cornell university 1975. and also he attended a seven week management programme in Harvard business school.





CAREER AND ACHIEVEMENTS


A​fter returning to India he gained so much experience in the Tata group of businesses and after he was taken charge as director of one of them in 1971 to the National Electronic and Co . A decade later in 1991 he became the chairman of tata industries.

During 21 year he led the Tata group revenues grew over 40 times and profit over 50 times . He got Tata tea to acquire tetley for $413.3 billion and purchased successfully truck-manufacturing operations of South Korea’s Daewoo Motors for $102 million . Tata steel completed a biggest corporate takeover after attaining the Anglo-dutch steel manufacturer Corus group for $11.3 billion in 2008 the tata motors purchase of elite british car brands jaguar and land rover from the ford motor company marked as largest ever accession by an indian automotive firm . The following year he launched the tiny rear engined and pod shaped vehicle TATA NANO for the middle class lives who dream to buy a car for their family for a cost they can afford with a starting price of $2000 after it was named as “people’s car”. in Tata’s words, would provide a “safe, affordable, all-weather form of transport” to millions of middle- and lower-income consumers.


PHILANTHROPIST


T​ata Education and Development Trust, a philanthropic affiliate of Tata Group, endowed a $28 million Tata Scholarship Fund that will allow Cornell University to provide financial aid to undergraduate students from India. The scholarship fund will support approximately 20 scholars at any given time and will ensure that the very best Indian students have access to Cornell, regardless of their financial circumstances. The scholarship will be awarded annually; recipients will receive the scholarship for the duration of their undergraduate study at Cornell. Tata Group, under the leadership of Ratan Tata formed the MIT Tata Center of Technology and Design at Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) with a mission to address the challenges of resource-constrained communities, with an initial focus on India




HONOURS AND AWARDS:


H​e has been awarded so many doctorates and ​ ​received the Padma Bhushan in 2000 and Padma Vibhushan in 2008, the third and second highest civilian honours awarded by the Government of India.

In December 2012 Tata retired as chairman of the Tata Group. He briefly served as interim chairman beginning in October 2016 then Cyrus mistry was appointed as chairman . Tata returned to retirement in January 2017 when Natarajan Chandrasekaran was appointed chairman of the Tata Group.

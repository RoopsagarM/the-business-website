The Pandemonium on India



Coronavirus disease 2019 formally known as COVID-19 was commenced in wuhan city, china. Since the cases have been profiliated and started spreading around the globe, then the official statement given by the Ministry of health(MOH) of the people republic of china is “ The new strain of coronavirus (novel) likely initiated in bats or mammals”.The World Health Organization (WHO) stated the definite symptoms caused by this pandemic disease are cough, cold, severe fever etc.

Outbreak

Transmission of the specific virus is very rapid when matched up to the other viruses and this circumstance was scarcely experienced by the people of wuhan. The ground of the wuhan turned as the graveyard. Later, the china govt came to know that the one and only way to control the situation is to lockdown the country. While the time passing out covid is transfused to the rest of the world. India is one of the affectees.

The impact on Inian Economy

The pandemic-covid19 has perturbed the global and indian economy unpleasantly.This virus has created a situation of pandemonium which occurs extremely rare. Besides afflicting people’s health it aggravated the economic growth of India.Giving prominence to the health of the citizens the Indian government has declared a stringent lock down nationwide in India.No one is allowed to do any kind of work other than the dire need ones for a period of 21 days and this has become as one of the factors that affected the economic conditions adversely.

Indian economy is expected to lose over ₹32,000 crore every day during the first 21-days of complete lockdown which was declared following the coronavirus outbreak.Up to 53% of businesses in the country will be significantly affected.

Initially there was a lack of clarity in streamlining what is an "essential" and what isn't. Especially, the people of informal sectors and the daily wage workers are more hazardous. Not only these the farmers who are growing perishables and the businesses like hotels and airlines are cutting the salaries and laying off employees.The live events industry has seen an estimated loss of ₹3,000 crore. World Bank and credit rating agencies have downgraded India's growth for fiscal year 2021 with the lowest figures India has seen in three decades since India's economic liberalization in the 1990s. Stock markets in India publicized their worst loss in history on 23 March 2020. The government of India declared various measures to tackle this situation. On 27 March the Reserve Bank of India also announced a number of measures which would make available ₹374,000 crores to the country's financial system. On 6th April a 30% salary cut for one year was announced for the President, Prime Minister and members of Parliament.

But suddenly the prime minister of India on 14th april announced that the lock down is going to be extended upto 3rd may. A new set of guidelines for the calibrated opening of the economy and relaxation of the lockdown were also set in place which will take effect from 20 April.

On 4th April, former RBI chief Raghuram Rajan said that the coronavirus pandemic in India may just be the "greatest emergency since Independence".

List of Famous Failed Businesses and startups in India – 2019


  Siri Manda




VIU:




Viu is a Hong Kong-based over-the-top (OTT) video streaming provider from PCCW Media, a subsidiary of PCCW.It had a significant presence in markets across Asia, Africa, and the Middle East. India was one of the critical markets for Viu because of the vertical growth expected in the video streaming industry in India.Viu at present has not shut operations in India, but they are soon going to wind up. Their top management has already left, and most of the employee strength is on notice.


Reasons for failure:


The primary reason for the failure of the company was the budget constraints. There was no way for the company to compete with giants like Netflix and Amazon Prime.



Doodhwala




Doodhwala worked to deliver milk and groceries directly to the doorsteps.The company offered a wide range of products ranging from milk to fruits and delivered the products before 7 AM daily. Doodhwala reduced their delivery cost to Rs.3, It positioned itself in a unique way in the competitive market where others struggled a lot.


Reasons for failure:


One of the biggest challenges facing Doodhwala was the presence of prominent players like BigBasket. It used Pune-based RainCan and Bengaluru-based Morningcart for its micro-delivery service. An online platform Fresh To Home gave assurance to Dhoodhwala subscribers that they will continue services.


Aditya Birla Idea Payments Bank (ABIPB):




Aditya Birla Payments Bank Limited (ABPB), which was launced on 2018 the fourth payment bank to get a license from RBI. Aditya Birla Nuvo Limited held 51 percent shares between Aditya Birla Nuvo Ltd. and Idea Cellular. The remaining 49 percent shares were with Idea Cellular.In 2019, Aditya Birla Payments Bank announced that it would shut operations to the receipt of requisite regulatory consents.


Reasons for failure:


In the observation by RBI, the reason for operating profit of such banks being negative was large capital expenditures involved in setting up infrastructure, leading to high operating expenses.Limitations, such as investment in government securities, which offer lesser returns as compared to other avenues such as mutual funds.


VodafoneM-Pesa:




Vodafone launched M-Pesa mobile money services in India in 2013.Vodafone Group said M-Pesa grew revenues by 20.7 percent to €0.75 billion which represented 12 percent of Emerging Consumer service revenues in 2019.The growth from M-Pesa helped Vodafone in supporting the telecom business which was developed in other countries like Africa.M-Pesa had more than 8.4 million customers in India, according to data on the Vodafone website dated in 2017.


Reasons for failure:


According to Vodafone Idea CEO, Balesh Sharma, regulatory changes for the payments bank business and deterioration in the health of the telecom sector were the key reasons for the failure.


Loanmeet:




Sunil Kumar co-founded Loanmeet after he realized that a large section of borrowers could not get personal and business loans from banks and other financial institutions due to lack of credit history, insufficient documentation, or other reasons.Loanmeet attempted to revolutionize banking at the grass-root level.LoanMeet used to finance working capital requirement, B2B marketplace financing, cash credit line, and channel financing in the range of Rs 5,000 to 5 lakh for short term period ranging from 15 days to 9 months.


Reasons for failure:


One of the prominent reasons for failure in lending space is that most of the lending companies are good at solving credit access problems.The lending market is an overcrowded market dominated by others, and Loanmeet couldn’t sustain the competition.As a result, it failed to raise further investment.

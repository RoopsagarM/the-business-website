Liberalised Agri-Trade







T​he best highlight of the package for agriculture announced by Union Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman is the decision to liberalise agricultural trade and marketing by creating a legal framework and mechanisms to give the farmer freedom and control over his produce. We all know that the manufacturer of soap can sell his soaps anywhere and a manufacturer of chairs can sell his chairs anywhere but why there is no freedom for the agriculture market. The main theme of these liberalised markets is that the farmer can sell his products anywhere without any restrictions. The existing restrictions on sale of agricultural commodities and the inter-state barriers in trade are to be done away with, and the idea of “one nation, one market’’ will in effect be extended to agriculture. The Agricultural Produce Marketing Committee controls the trade currently, and farmers have to sell their produce there. Once the new legal framework is in place, they will be free to sell their produce to anyone, anywhere in India, and not to just one licensee. This proves theoretically correct but one should know the state of our agriculture sector.The farmer who produces should get a fair price andFood security for the common man. Farmers need income and people need food security. What's going to happen in practice? We know most of the indian farmers are having less than 5 acre or even without land but doing agriculture on lease without proper lease agreement with LandLord and he uses most of the crop for his utility and rest he may sell in the markets .



How can he go and sell in other states or how he can store his goods ? He can't store his goods either in houses or in farm lands ,In Fact there are no proper cold warehouses in villages . Now India is not only giving chance for commision agents to sell in markets but Now it is also giving chance to sell to Multinational Companies, reputed Indian corporates like Reliance to procure & sell their goods directly ,But due to this most of the small farmers may won’t get benefit.These companies procure from farmers at lower price with having facility of storage are able to sell at higher prices at the appropriate time.This causes great loss to the farmer and he will never get a fair chance to sell his goods at fair price. For example if we consider an onion farmer he will be paid , say 5 rs a kilo but an ultimate consumer is paying 30 rs per kilo in the market because of the involvement of commission agents. If multinational companies involved in the picture think about the ultimate price of the onion in the market! . Naturally MNCs with an international market never owe to our national food security they sell at a good price around the world wherever it is ..... to get higher profit . History has witnessed food starvation in latin american countries as american MNCs used the food grains for pet dogs . Thus the fate of the Indian farmers is being bet in the hands of MNCs.
